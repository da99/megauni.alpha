Over-paid Suits

"They aren't paying top dollar for experts. They're paying top dollar for the appearance of experts.

My sister graduated from an Ivy League school, and a lot of her former college buddies are now working at these sorts of consulting firms. The basic gig is that these kids are paid very very well to travel around the country flaunting their Ivy League credentials and giving PowerPoint presentations to various bigwigs about topics they know little-to-nothing about."

From: http://thedailywtf.com/Comments/Benched.aspx#312081

"I saw too many times when an "expert" was sent in to work and spent their time learning the technology while billing the client. And I think we've seen a lot of stories of that on this site as well."

From: http://thedailywtf.com/Comments/Benched.aspx?pg=4#312253

The friend I mentioned above had only listed C++ and Java on his resume, because he was weak in his other languages. However, they sent him on numerous other assignments; he quickly learned he needed to ask what the main tech for the job was going to be, so if needed he could buy books at the airport, and read them on the plane. Not enough time to become a master, but sufficient time to become much more skilled than some of the conslutants I've had the mispleasure of working with.

From: http://thedailywtf.com/Comments/Benched.aspx?pg=4#312347
